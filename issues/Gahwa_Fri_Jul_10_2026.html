What happened
The Strait of Hormuz nearly stopped moving.
Strait of Hormuz traffic slowed to a crawl Thursday as US airstrikes hit Iran for a second straight day and President Trump declared the ceasefire 'over' at the NATO summit in Ankara. A Saudi-flagged crude tanker was attacked, and business newsletters report that retaliatory strikes hit military bases in Kuwait and Bahrain.
Saudi Arabia is struggling to revive oil flows from Ras Tanura as tanker shortages and sky-high freight costs bite.

Saudi German Health elected a new seven-member board with no members of the Batterjee family after a financial reporting probe. UAE officials are accelerating plans to move trade away from the Strait of Hormuz, aiming for a 'zero Hormuz' logistics corridor. GCC Opportunity
Hormuz-Bypass Logistics Corp.
The gapGulf trade is hostage to a single 21-nautical-mile-wide strait that is now a warzone, and no viable multimodal alternative exists today.
The insightWar-forced urgency means governments and insurers will finally underwrite the capex that peacetime always deemed too expensive.
The playBuild a bonded multimodal corridor from Fujairah through Saudi Arabia to the Mediterranean, combining rail, pipeline, and dry-port infrastructure.
The businessTolling fees, logistics-as-a-service for exporters, and land-lease income along the corridor; primary customers are Gulf NOCs, global commodity traders, and shipping lines.
Why nowUAE officials are already accelerating ‘zero Hormuz’ plans and US-Iran hostilities make the risk of doing nothing quantifiable for the first time.
Why GCC winsThe GCC possesses a contiguous land bridge between the Gulf and the Red Sea/Mediterranean that no other major oil-exporting region can replicate, plus sovereign capital to fund it.
The moatSecuring cross-border wayleaves, unified customs, and sovereign guarantees across multiple GCC states is a political-engineering feat that a private competitor cannot duplicate.
Priced inThe war ends quickly, Hormuz reopens, and the economic case for the corridor collapses before ground is broken.
Not priced in The conflict drags on so long that the corridor becomes the new permanent artery, unlocking a logistics real-estate boom that few have priced in.
