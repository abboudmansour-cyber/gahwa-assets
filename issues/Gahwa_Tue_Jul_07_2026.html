What happened
Almarai's margins shrink despite growth
Profit fell 2% even as revenue jumped 11%, squeezed by higher feed and energy costs from the Iran conflict. The dairy giant's margins are being eroded by exactly the kind of logistics chaos Gulf importers now face daily.
The Q2 earnings release this week showed rising costs outpacing topline growth, a direct read on how the Strait of Hormuz disruption feeds into corporate profits.

Morning Question
​
Today's question
Who will manage the Strait of Hormuz,Oman or Iran?
Two rival management plans are on the table for the world's most critical oil chokepoint, which has been impassable in stretches since the US-Iran war began. Shipping firms now face a strategic fork that could redefine Gulf transport corridors.
At stake: Global crude flows and LNG supply chains could split between two rival regimes.
The answer
Oman and Iran have advanced competing proposals, with Oman likely backing a multilateral oversight framework while Iran seeks to assert sovereign control over passage, per an Asia Group report. No single plan has won broad acceptance yet, and vessels are navigating cautiously as the two visions collide.
