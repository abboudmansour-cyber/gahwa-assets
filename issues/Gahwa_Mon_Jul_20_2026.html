The Lead
​
A single Saudi tanker loaded all day while Iran shot at ships in Hormuz.
Saudi crude loadings from Gulf terminals have collapsed, with only a single tanker berthed at Ras Tanura, and total exports near 4.6 million barrels per day, down from 7.3 million bpd before the war. Brent crude surged 6.5% to $90.25.

The IRGC, quoted by Middle East Eye, warned that 'the waterway will not be safe for oil and gas passage as long as US actions continue.' Capital Economics warned that simultaneous disruption of Hormuz and Bab Al Mandeb could tip the global economy into recession. Iraq’s exports are slowing from tanker shortages, and Houthi drones have threatened Saudi’s Abha airport and oil facilities.

For the GCC, this is an existential supply-chain moment: the Strait handles a fifth of the world’s oil, and the region’s budget plans, from Vision 2030 mega-projects to the UAE’s non-oil trade push, all assume open sea lanes.
Assess shipping insurance and alternative export routes immediately; monitor Houthi threats to Bab Al Mandeb.
The Rundown
​
​
GCC & SAUDI
PIF's $55B EA Deal Nears EU Approval
The EU preliminary merger review ends July 22, with a full subsidies verdict due by July 30.

JPMorgan has already placed $15 billion in debt for the deal, drawing over $50 billion in investor demand. The acquisition is the centerpiece of Saudi Arabia’s gaming push and would give the Kingdom a dominant seat at the table of global entertainment. The PIF, together with Affinity Partners and Silver Lake, is betting that owning EA’s FIFA, Battlefield, and The Sims franchises is cheaper than building a homegrown gaming giant from scratch. With Saudi contract awards up 160% last quarter, the message is clear: the kingdom is buying, not waiting. Watch the EU’s final ruling on July 30; a yes unlocks the largest Saudi overseas acquisition ever.
​
AI & TECH
Saudi AI Startup Think Raises Record $8M
Riyadh-based Think has landed $8 million in what it claims is the largest AI infrastructure pre-seed round in MENA, co-led by RAED Ventures and Wa’ed Ventures. Founded in 2025 by Ahmed AlSharif and Ammar Enaya, Think builds integrated AI hardware and orchestration software that sustained GPU utilisation above 90% in benchmarks, versus an industry average of 30, 50%, and delivered per-million-token costs some 10 times cheaper than frontier models.

The cash will scale manufacturing, expand the team, and push deployments across Saudi Arabia and the wider GCC over the next 18 months. The round signals that local money is getting serious about owning the compute layer, not just renting it. For a region often accused of importing AI, Think is an argument that the next data center might run on home-built stacks. For VCs: early-stage AI hardware is the new frontier; follow Think’s manufacturing milestones for deal signals.
​
GCC & SAUDI
Saudi Reclaims GCC Projects Crown, +160%
Saudi Arabia surged back to the top of the Gulf’s projects market in the second quarter, with contract awards jumping 160.4% quarter-on-quarter to $30 billion, according to data from Kamco. The haul accounts for more than half of the $59.4 billion awarded across the GCC.

Big-ticket items included a $4.6 billion defence ministry deal for Al Bawani and a $1.3 billion road contract in Kuwait. Construction awards rose 184% year-on-year and gas projects quintupled to $4.4 billion. The figures underscore Vision 2030’s momentum even as regional conflict raises insurance costs. With $2 trillion of planned and unawarded projects in the pipeline, Saudi is set to keep outspending everyone. Infrastructure and defence contractors should watch for Phase 2 awards from Diriyah and the MoD pipeline.
​
CULTURE
Quba Mosque Wins 10x Expansion Tender
Saudi Arabia is putting the Prophet’s first mosque on a massive growth path: a new tender will expand Quba’s footprint from 5,000 square meters to 53,000 square meters, boosting capacity from 12,000 to 66,000 worshippers.

The project is one of several religious tourism upgrades timed to absorb rising pilgrim numbers. With Umrah visas up 22.5% year-on-year to 931,500 and over 1.27 million pilgrims already arriving, the expansion is a supply-side bet that faith-based travel will continue to be a reliable cashless corridor. The tender also signals that sacred heritage is treated as serious public infrastructure, not just spiritual legacy. Construction and fit-out firms should track this and similar mosque expansion tenders launching across the Kingdom.
​
PRODUCT
UAE Build Costs Jump 5%, Squeezing Margins
The cost of building in the UAE rose 5.2% year-on-year in Q1, with raw materials up over 8%, block works nearly 25% dearer, and finishing materials in Abu Dhabi up 11% quarter-on-quarter, according to the latest tender price data.

Alec Holdings saw building margins flat at 6.8%, and its energy division margin collapsed to 2.9% from 8.7%. Land prices in Dubai are up 21% over six years to a median AED 5.9 million. With total construction costs projected to rise another 4.5% this year and supply chains disrupted by the Iran conflict, mid-to-large project margins have shrunk to 8, 12%. The boom is starting to feel expensive. Review cost contingency budgets on active projects and hedge material inputs now.
