What happened
Washington answered with missiles.
Oil prices jumped 6% to $75 a barrel, tanker rates surged, and the US Navy said it was working to keep the waterway open despite Tehran’s announcement.

The strait handles about 20 million barrels of oil per day. A sustained closure would force Gulf exporters to divert through longer routes or idle wells. Saudi Arabia has kept flows moving through the East-West pipeline, but spare capacity is thin. Fitch had just affirmed Saudi’s A+ rating with a 0.6% GDP forecast for 2026; a prolonged Hormuz shutdown would slash that.

For Gulf operators, the return to active hostilities means higher insurance premiums, potential convoy delays, and a scramble to lock in supply contracts before prices move further. The last time Iran threatened the strait, the US and Gulf navies quickly restored calm. This time, Trump’s ‘overwhelming force’ rhetoric suggests a longer game.
Book oil hedges this week and communicate alternative supply routes to your logistics teams.

GCC & SAUDI
Carney Lands $1B in Saudi Deals
Canadian PM Mark Carney’s first visit to Jeddah in 26 years yielded 13 agreements and MoUs worth over $1 billion, with mining and infrastructure at the center.

The Royal Commission for Riyadh City signed two contracts worth $440 million, and Maaden locked in a $700 million engineering partnership with Canada’s Hatch for its $110 billion mining expansion. Ivanhoe Electric and Maaden extended their exploration joint venture across 50,000 square kilometers of the Arabian Shield through 2033.

The deals signal that Saudi’s mineral wealth, estimated at $2.5 trillion, needs foreign technical muscle to get out of the ground. Mining service companies and equipment suppliers should review the new partnership for supplier registration openings.
​
GCC & SAUDI
Saudi Tourist Visa Bundle Drops
Saudi Arabia launched a bundled tourist visa for seven nationalities, Jordan, Egypt, India, Bangladesh, Indonesia, Mexico, and Pakistan, packages starting at SAR 4,000 per adult.

The Tourism Ministry’s move aims to boost visitor numbers after a slow year marked by regional tensions. The visa covers multiple entries and is designed to streamline travel for families and groups, cutting the red tape that previously required separate applications.

For hotel operators and tour companies, the bundle should open up new demand from South Asia and Egypt, two markets that already send millions of workers and pilgrims to the Kingdom annually. The timing suggests Riyadh is pushing to meet Vision 2030 tourism targets despite global headwinds. Tour operators should package short-haul experiences for the Indian and Egyptian markets before the next season.
​
GCC & SAUDI
e& Exits Vodafone, Niel In
UAE telecom group e& is selling its 16.2% stake in Vodafone to French billionaire Xavier Niel for about $6 billion, making him the largest shareholder of the European carrier.

The sale unwinds a three-year bet that Vodafone could turn around under pressure. e& had accumulated the position in 2022-23 but never gained board seats or operational influence. Niel, a serial infrastructure investor, now plans to push for consolidation in Europe.

For the Gulf, the deal recycles e& capital back home, likely into AI, data centers, or further African expansion. It also shows that Gulf sovereign-linked firms are willing to exit quietly when a trophy asset no longer fits the strategy. Reallocate portfolio to UAE tech infrastructure plays; e& is likely to redeploy cash domestically.
​
CULTURE
LIV Golf’s Last Dance
As the 154th Open Championship tees off at Royal Birkdale, LIV Golf is fighting for survival: Saudi PIF funding ends after this season, and the fledgling league has filed a WARN notice for potential mass layoffs.

Bryson DeChambeau’s contract expires soon, and the two-time US Open champ has missed the cut in every major this year. LIV has just four events left and is scrambling for new investors, yet 16 LIV players qualified for The Open, including Victor Perez via a co-sanctioned event.

Saudi Arabia’s bet on golf disrupted the sport but never turned a profit. The PGA Tour is preparing a two-series model for 2028, and the window for a merger is closing. For Gulf sports investors, LIV is a cautionary tale: you can buy talent, but not a loyal audience. If you hold LIV hospitality or sponsorship ties, begin contingency planning for a post-PIF structure.

MACRO & GEO
OPEC+ Boosts Quotas by 188k bbl/d
The cartel raised production quotas by 188,000 barrels per day in July, a modest loosening as demand wobbles and the Hormuz crisis threatens supply.

The move comes as oil prices swing sharply. The real test comes when the physical market sees whether Saudi crude can reach Asia. Factor in wider Gulf shipping spreads when pricing forward contracts.
