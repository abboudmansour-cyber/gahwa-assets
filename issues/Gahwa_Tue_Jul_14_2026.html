What happened
Iranian missiles reportedly struck two UAE tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, killing one crew member, the first direct hit on Emirati vessels since the latest escalation began.
According to the UAE Ministry of Defence, two UAE-flagged tankers were struck by Iranian cruise missiles in the southern lane of the Strait of Hormuz on Monday, killing one Indian crew member and wounding eight others. Reportedly, the attack occurred in Omani territorial waters; both vessels caught fire before crews brought the blazes under control. The strike came hours after the US launched fresh airstrikes on 140 Iranian military targets and President Trump reinstated a naval blockade on Iranian ports. Trump also said Washington would act as “guardian” of the strait and seek a 20 percent reimbursement on all non-Iranian cargo transiting the waterway. Iran reportedly retaliated by firing three waves of missiles and drones at Gulf nations, including Qatar, Kuwait, Jordan, Oman and Bahrain.

Brent crude jumped 9 percent to $83.30 a barrel before topping $85, while Gulf markets slumped. Only six ships crossed the strait on Sunday, the lowest in five weeks. The escalation directly hits UAE trade arteries, threatens the region’s tourism recovery, and will force every logistics operator in the GCC to re-price risk immediately. Shipping insurers are already quoting war-risk premiums that will cascade into everything from food imports to construction materials.
Monitor Red Sea and Arabian Gulf freight rates and force-majeure clauses in Gulf supply contracts this week.

GCC & SAUDI
Burjeel Targets 10 Saudi Surgery Hubs
Burjeel Holdings is committing roughly $100 million to build its first two specialised day-surgery centres in Saudi Arabia, with the first opening in Khobar by early 2027 and plans to scale to 10 facilities nationwide. Chairman and CEO Shamsheer Vayalil confirmed the investment after completing a $500 million sukuk that was 3.2 times oversubscribed.

The move capitalises on Saudi Arabia’s drive to shift elective procedures away from crowded general hospitals, aligning with Vision 2030’s privatisation and healthcare efficiency goals. Day surgery margins typically run higher than inpatient care, and Burjeel already operates a profitable high-throughput model in the UAE.

For regional healthcare operators, the Bet is clear: Saudi demand for affordable, quick-turnaround surgery is set to explode. Burjeel is racing to lock in prime catchment areas before competitors like Saudi German Health or international chains do the same. Watch for real estate and staffing partnerships in Khobar and Riyadh as Burjeel moves to secure clinical talent.

FINTECH & CRYPTO
Saudi SPACs Get Green Light
Saudi Arabia’s Capital Market Authority has opened the door to SPAC listings on Tadawul with a new regulatory framework, according to officials. The move gives blank-check firms a clear path onto the region’s largest exchange just as US SPAC volumes dwindle.

For Gulf family offices and investment groups sitting on dry powder, this offers an alternative exit and fundraising vehicle that can funnel capital into late-stage tech, healthcare, and logistics assets across the Kingdom. Advisers say the first SPAC filings could land before year-end.

The framework arrives as Tadawul looks to deepen its equity market beyond banks and petrochemicals, and it gives the GCC its first regulated SPAC venue outside the ADGM. Expect regional private equity and venture capital firms to begin structuring Saudi-targeted SPACs this quarter.

AI & TECH
UAE Gets Unrestricted AI Chip Access
The US Commerce Department has moved the UAE into Country Group A:5, eliminating the licensing bottleneck for advanced AI chips, satellite components, and defence equipment. G42 and Core42 are listed as approved recipients, and Abu Dhabi’s MGX will now receive favourable reviews for chip and server licence applications.

The decision effectively removes the 500,000-chip ceiling that Washington had imposed, according to filings. US-headquartered firms such as Microsoft, OpenAI, and Oracle with UAE operations also fall under the new waiver.

This cements the UAE’s role as Washington’s privileged AI partner in the Gulf and gives G42 a clear advantage in building the region’s largest compute cluster. The approval lasts 270 days absent further notice. Map which cloud and data centre projects can now accelerate their chip procurement timelines in the UAE.

Riyadh Crowns a Japanese-Peruvian Fusion Spot
Crowne Plaza Riyadh RDC Hotel has opened KOI, a signature restaurant blending Japanese precision with Peruvian flavours, the latest upscale addition to the capital’s dining scene. The outlet targets the corporate and diplomatic crowd at the convention centre complex.

For HORECA operators, KOI is another data point that Riyadh’s high-end dining demand is growing faster than the supply of international concepts, especially those that fuse cuisines in novel ways. The city is absorbing a wave of premium openings, yet occupancy and cover counts remain strong in the luxury tier. Restaurant groups should track booking data from KOI to size the market for Asian-Latin fusion concepts in Dammam and Jeddah.

GCC & SAUDI
Mortgage Fix Targets Saudi Housing Slump
New residential mortgages fell 56 percent year-on-year to SAR 4.4 billion in May, prompting the Real Estate Development Fund to launch a programme with SNB and the National Housing Company offering instalments from SAR 699 a month. Building permits jumped 28 percent in April as citizens shifted to self-build.

The mortgage plunge, coupled with warnings from Knight Frank of a high-end oversupply, pressures lenders to find qualified borrowers. The new programme tries to pull first-time buyers back by promising ultra-low monthly payments, but the credit appetite of banks remains constrained by higher interest rates. Track mortgage origination data for Q3 to see if the programme moves the needle before Knight Frank’s oversupply timeline bites.
