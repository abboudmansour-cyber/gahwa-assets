GCC & SAUDI
UAE Loses Q2 Projects Crown
New project awards in the UAE plummeted 56.6% quarter-on-quarter, dropping the Emirates from the top GCC projects spot to second place behind Saudi Arabia, data from Kamco Invest shows. The total value of contracts awarded in the UAE last quarter was $20.5 billion, a sharp reversal from the record pace that had put it ahead of the Kingdom in Q1.

The swing reflects Saudi Vision 2030 momentum: giga-projects and the 2034 World Cup build-out are pulling contract volumes into the Kingdom, even as UAE developers brace for a softer real estate cycle. Dubai's residential values fell another 1% in June, extending the post-February slide to 10%, yet ready-home transactions surged 46.8%, signalling buyers are hunting deals.

For contractors and suppliers, this redraws the regional opportunity map. Saudi now commands both scale and urgency; the UAE pipeline remains large but is pivoting toward smaller, quicker-turnaround residential and infrastructure upgrades rather than multi-billion-dollar master-planned launches. Reallocate business development resources toward Saudi infrastructure bids this quarter.

FINTECH & CRYPTO
UAE Tightens Foreign Bank Rules
The Central Bank of the UAE has tightened regulations governing representative offices of foreign banks, effectively restricting the onboarding support they can offer Indian lenders looking to serve non-resident Indian customers in the Emirates. The move comes amid friction between the CBUAE and the Reserve Bank of India over Indian authorities pulling USD deposits from NRIs in the UAE.

The new rules could slow the expansion of Indian banks' deposit-raising activities in the UAE, a corridor that moves billions of dollars annually. For Gulf-based Indian nationals and businesses, it may make cross-border banking more expensive and less seamless just as the RBI pushes harder for rupee settlement of Gulf trade. Banks should review rep-office licenses and reassess client onboarding flows before September.

AI & TECH
AVELIN AI Raises $3.7M Pre-Seed
UAE-based AVELIN AI closed a $3.7 million pre-seed round to scale its sovereign AI platform, targeting government and enterprise clients that want to run large language models on infrastructure fully ringfenced within national borders. The round, reported by Wamda, signals growing investor appetite for AI that doesn't send data to US hyperscalers.

The company's pitch, data sovereignty and model control, aligns directly with GCC governments that are drafting AI regulations demanding local data residency and auditability. AVELIN's platform claims to cut the time to deploy a sovereign AI stack from months to weeks.

As the region's cloud market balloons past $10 billion, sovereign AI startups are carving a niche between the big American cloud providers and expensive on-premise builds. Meet Gulf AI startups focused on data sovereignty; demand is rising fast.
​
CULTURE
Sheikha Bodour Awarded Italy's Top Honor
Sheikha Bodour bint Sultan Al Qasimi has been awarded the officer rank of the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic, Italy's highest state honour, in recognition of her work strengthening cultural ties between the UAE and Italy. The award was presented by Ambassador Lorenzo Fanara in Sharjah. Her children's book “The House of Wisdom” was translated into Italian, and she helped rebuild the historic Giannino Stoppani children's bookshop in Bologna.

The recognition underscores the soft power of Gulf cultural diplomacy at a moment when Italy is deepening economic and energy ties with the UAE. Sheikha Bodour's work bridges literary heritage and modern publishing, reinforcing Sharjah's bid to be a global book capital. Watch for a rise in Gulf-Europe cultural collaborations and literary translation programs.

FINTECH & CRYPTO
Keyper Raises $11M for Rent Tech
Dubai-based proptech startup Keyper raised an $11 million Series A to digitise the UAE rental market, launching a platform that streamlines payments, contract management, and tenant-landlord interactions. The round, reported by Wamda, highlights growing investment in Gulf real-estate tech as the market softens.

With Dubai rents forecast to dip further, landlords are under pressure to retain tenants, making frictionless digital rental experiences a retention tool. Keyper already handles thousands of units and plans to expand into Saudi Arabia's far larger rental market. Explore proptech investment opportunities; rental market digitization has room to run.
