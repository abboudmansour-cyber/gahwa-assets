The Lead

US Strikes Iran, Gulf States Hit
Brent crude surged 9.4% to $92.12 a barrel, and Goldman Sachs warned that oil could jump to $120 if the Strait of Hormuz remains disrupted.

The Pentagon disclosed that the US cost of the Iran campaign has reached $37.5 billion, as Defense Secretary Hegseth faced skeptical lawmakers. For Gulf businesses, the immediate toll is visible in spiking shipping insurance, slashed advertising budgets, and stalled hiring, though Saudi FDI and non-oil growth remain rare bright spots.
Shipping and logistics firms must reroute contingency plans immediately; traders should hedge oil supply risk.
The Rundown
​
​​
GCC & SAUDI
UAE Hiring Shrinks First Time in Four Years
UAE hiring activity fell 4% quarter-on-quarter in Q2 2026, the first contraction in more than four years, as regional conflict and falling PMI rattled employer confidence. Cooper Fitch's survey shows GCC-wide hiring fell 3%, with Qatar down 6%, Kuwait and UAE down 4% each, while Saudi Arabia and Oman eked out 1% growth.

The slide is not uniform: finance and AI-data roles expanded, but software and cloud hiring dipped. The share of UAE postings requiring AI skills tripled since 2021, yet the PMI slipped to 50.8, its weakest reading in over five years.

For operators, the data signals a tightening market where hiring freezes after late-stage recruitment; companies should lock in key hires now before competition for AI-savvy talent intensifies. Companies should accelerate offers to secure scarce AI-savvy talent before the freeze deepens.
​​
FINTECH & CRYPTO
SILQ Lands $20M for Saudi SME Finance
A $20 million Shariah-compliant structured facility will scale SILQ's embedded finance platform Fina to more than 50,000 Saudi businesses.

The financing from Gemcorp Capital marks the firm's first direct lending, first Shariah structure, and first mezzanine deal in Saudi Arabia, underscoring how international capital is chasing Vision 2030's small-business push. SILQ, formed from the merger of Sary and ShopUp, already enabled over SAR 10 billion in B2B commerce this year.

The cash will expand invoice financing and trade finance for SMEs starved of credit; with hiring stalling elsewhere, this is a rare pocket of growth for operators serving the Saudi SME backbone. International investors eye Saudi fintech; similar platforms should explore structured financing to bypass traditional lending bottlenecks.
​​
AI & TECH
UAE Sets Up AI Data Watchdog with Teeth
The new UAE Artificial Intelligence and Data Authority merges three government entities and reports directly to cabinet, putting teeth behind the Personal Data Protection Law that has gathered dust since 2022.

Companies must now demonstrate how data moves across their business, how AI uses it, and who is accountable, or risk investigations and administrative penalties. The fine schedule is not yet published, but governance is no longer optional.

For every business in the UAE rolling up AI pilots, this means the playbook just shifted from move-fast to document-everything. Compliance officers should begin mapping data flows and AI usage before the first enforcement action lands.
​​
CULTURE
UAE Women Swap HIIT for Rest
A new wellness narrative is taking hold in the UAE, swapping 5am CrossFit and Barry's Bootcamp for recovery lounges, sleep protocols, and digital detoxes.

Emirates Woman profiles what it calls 'The Rest Revolution,' arguing that next-gen luxury lifestyle is defined not by exhaustion but by restoration. The trend aligns with a global shift: IWSR data shows even Gen Z drinking rates are flat, not declining, but wellness spending continues to rise.

For hospitality and retail operators, the signal is that high-spending consumers increasingly value spa memberships, meditation apps, and low-intensity leisure over traditional high-adrenaline attractions. Hotels and gyms should diversify recovery and mindfulness offerings to capture the shift.

Tawal Eyes 10,000 Mobily Towers
Adding Mobily's portfolio would give it the scale to dominate Saudi 5G infrastructure.

For telecom operators and investors, the consolidation accelerates a regional tower asset playbook, turning passive infrastructure into tradable equity. Investors should track the IPO pipeline as tower assets become the next GCC infrastructure play.
The AI Race
​
Origen and Deepexi team up for MEA AI
Abu Dhabi's Origen and China’s publicly listed Deepexi announced a partnership at the WAIC conference to push enterprise AI from pilot to production across the Middle East and Africa. Origen brings AI-native infrastructure and government transformation expertise; Deepexi adds enterprise AI and agent technologies, targeting Arabic-language models and private cloud deployments with initial rollouts in Q4.
​Gulf angle
The deal gives Gulf enterprises a ready-made path to advanced AI without ceding data sovereignty to US hyperscalers, right as the UAE tightens its own AI governance.
