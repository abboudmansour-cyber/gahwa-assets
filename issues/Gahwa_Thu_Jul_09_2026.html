Signals to Know

​
MACRO & GEO
IMF cuts GCC growth forecast to 0.7%
The IMF slashed its 2026 Middle East growth forecast by 1.2 percentage points to 0.7%, assuming Strait of Hormuz traffic only partially resumes by mid-July, with Iraq, Kuwait, and Qatar contracting sharply. The downgrade landed Wednesday alongside fresh hostilities, reinforcing the economic cost of the failed ceasefire.
​
FINTECH & CRYPTO
Saudi-UAE bank transfers blocked
Cross-border payments between the Gulf's two largest economies are suddenly seizing up, with transfers from Saudi to UAE delayed, returned, or blocked since at least May, hitting $25.7 bn in bilateral trade. With no official explanation and trade worth billions affected, the blockage is escalating alongside a broader political rift.
​
AI & TECH
Saudi AI firms raise $9.1 bn
AI companies in Saudi Arabia have secured $9.1 billion in funding, as government spending on emerging tech jumped over 56% last year, cementing the kingdom's 14th place in the Global AI Index. The data emerged this week alongside Salam's new research centre in Al Ahsa, intensifying the race for sovereign AI.
​
CULTURE
Abu Dhabi Biennial returns in October
Abu Dhabi's public art push kicks off November 15 as the Biennial returns with free-to-view commissions, led by ex-Macba director Elvira Dyangani Ose under the theme 'Home: A Glossary for a Communal Sense of Place.' The dates and artistic director were announced this week, marking the next phase of Saadiyat Island's cultural expansion.
​
GCC & SAUDI
Khorfakkan container traffic surges 8x
Container traffic at Sharjah's Khorfakkan terminal jumped from 8,000 to 65,000 boxes per week, as the Hormuz crisis forced ships to the UAE's Indian Ocean coast, driving a $2 bn expansion. The surge figures, disclosed this week, reveal how Gulf ports are redrawing logistics maps almost overnight.
