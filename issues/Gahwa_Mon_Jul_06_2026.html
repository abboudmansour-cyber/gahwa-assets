Today's Signal

What happened
The UAE's non-oil PMI just hit 50.8 — its weakest June in five years — while Saudi's jumped to a four-month high. The Gulf’s economic currents are pulling in opposite directions.
The UAE's non-oil PMI plunged to 50.8 in June, the weakest reading in over five years, and firms cut jobs at the sharpest pace since August 2020.
The slide below 51 puts immediate pressure on hiring and budget forecasts for H2.

Signals to Know

MACRO & GEO
OPEC+ Adds 188K Barrels for August
Opec+ approved a fifth consecutive monthly output increase of 188,000 bpd, putting nearly 800,000 bpd back on the market since April. The gradual unwinding, combined with demand concerns, pushed Brent crude to $72, the lowest since the conflict began.
​
MACRO & GEO
Saudi Non-Oil PMI Hits Four-Month High
Saudi Arabia’s non-oil PMI climbed to 53.3 in June, the highest since February, as domestic demand rebounded and business confidence soared. The divergence with the UAE’s 50.8 reading makes capital allocation decisions urgent this week.
​
AI & TECH
BlueFive Co-Leads $3B Kling AI Round
Abu Dhabi’s BlueFive Capital co-led a $3 billion funding round for Chinese AI video startup Kling AI, valuing the company at $18 billion. The deal signals that Gulf capital is not waiting for a ceasefire to place big AI bets.
​
CULTURE
World Cup Quarters: UAE Kick-off Times
UAE viewers are planning late nights as the World Cup 2026 quarter-finals kick off this week, with kick-off times ranging from 8pm to 3am. Restaurants, shisha cafes and sports bars are extending hours and staffing up for the midnight matches.
