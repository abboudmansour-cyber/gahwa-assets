Morning Question
​
Today's question
Is the Hormuz shipping detour around Oman becoming permanent?
Vessel-tracking data shows nearly a third of crossings now use the coastal route, and GPS jamming continues. War-risk insurance spikes are making the direct path uninsurable for many tankers.
At stake: Oman could capture maritime revenue that once belonged to Iran and the UAE.
The answer
Yes, it is likely structural. The pattern solidified over the weekend and ship captains are actively avoiding the direct path. As long as the fragile truce holds but GPS interference persists, the detour becomes the new default route.
